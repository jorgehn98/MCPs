# LinkedIn Post Best Practices

Load this file when the user asks you to write, improve, or create a LinkedIn post.

---

## The anatomy of a high-performing LinkedIn post

### 1. Hook (first 1-2 lines)

This is everything. LinkedIn shows only the first ~200 characters before the "see more" cutoff.
The hook must make the reader stop scrolling.

**Effective hook patterns:**
- Counterintuitive statement: "I turned down a $500K offer. Here's why."
- Bold number/result: "We grew from 0 to 10,000 users in 3 months. Here's exactly how."
- Direct question: "Why do most startups fail in year two?"
- Relatable pain: "I spent 2 years building something nobody wanted."
- Story opener: "Two years ago I was about to quit. Then one thing changed."

**Avoid:**
- Starting with "I'm excited to share..."
- Starting with "Thrilled to announce..."
- Weak openers like "Today I want to talk about..."

---

### 2. Body structure

**Short paragraphs.** 1-2 sentences max per paragraph. Use white space aggressively — walls of text get skipped.

**Common formats that perform well:**

**The story arc:**
```
Hook (problem or moment)

What happened / what I did

What I learned

The lesson for others
```

**The list:**
```
Hook

Here are 5 things I wish I knew:

1. [Point]
2. [Point]
3. [Point]
4. [Point]
5. [Point]

The one that changed everything: [#X]
```

**The before/after:**
```
Hook

Before: [situation]
After: [result]

Here's what made the difference:
[explanation]
```

---

### 3. CTA (call to action)

End with something that invites engagement. Options:
- "What would you have done?"
- "Have you experienced this? Drop it in the comments."
- "Save this if you found it useful."
- "Follow me for more on [topic]."

Avoid asking for likes directly — it feels cheap. Asking for opinions or stories works better.

---

## Length

| Post type | Optimal length |
|-----------|---------------|
| Story / personal | 150-300 words |
| Tip / insight | 80-150 words |
| List | 100-250 words |
| Thought leadership | 200-400 words |

**Rule:** Every sentence should earn its place. If a line doesn't add value, cut it.

---

## Hashtags

- Use 3-5 hashtags maximum
- Place them at the end of the post
- Mix broad (#marketing) with niche (#b2bsaas)
- Avoid over-stuffing — it looks spammy

---

## Formatting tips

- Use line breaks between every paragraph
- Use bold sparingly for emphasis (not supported in plain LinkedIn text, but you can use Unicode bold or ALL CAPS for key words)
- Emojis work well as bullet replacements or to add tone — but use max 3-5 per post
- Numbers and data make claims credible: "83% of" beats "most"

---

## What performs best by content type

| Content | Why it works |
|---------|-------------|
| Personal story with lesson | Emotional + educational |
| Contrarian take on industry norm | Debate drives comments |
| Transparent failure / lesson | Vulnerability = relatability |
| Step-by-step how-to | Saves as reference |
| Behind-the-scenes | Curiosity and authenticity |
| Data/research with your take | Authority + shareability |

---

## What to avoid

- Vague motivational content ("Work hard, dream big") — zero value, low engagement
- Humble bragging without substance
- Posting just to post — frequency matters less than quality
- Tagging people who aren't relevant just to get visibility
- Asking for engagement in the first comment (LinkedIn may suppress this)

---

## Image and video posts

**Images:**
- Single striking image outperforms carousels for reach
- Text overlays on images stop the scroll
- Aspect ratio: 1:1 (square) or 4:5 for mobile

**Videos:**
- Add captions — most people watch without sound
- First 3 seconds must hook the viewer
- 30-90 seconds is the sweet spot for reach
- Native uploads outperform YouTube/Vimeo links significantly
