# X (Twitter) Tweet Best Practices

Load this file when the user asks you to write, improve, draft, or create a tweet or thread.

---

## The anatomy of a high-performing tweet

### 1. Hook (the only sentence that matters at first)

X shows the full tweet in most contexts, but the first 8-12 words decide if someone stops scrolling or keeps going.

**Effective opening patterns:**
- Bold claim: "Most people misunderstand how AI actually works."
- Counterintuitive take: "Speed is overrated. Here's what actually compounds."
- Open loop: "I read 200 papers on sleep. One insight changed everything."
- Hard number: "We went from $0 to $2M ARR in 11 months. The playbook:"
- Direct question: "Why do most startups fail right after their best quarter?"
- Controversial but defensible: "Remote work didn't kill productivity. Bad management did."

**Avoid:**
- Starting with "I" (weak, self-centered opening)
- Vague intros like "So I've been thinking about..."
- "Thread 🧵" as the only hook — lead with substance first

---

### 2. Tweet length

| Goal | Optimal length |
|------|---------------|
| Maximum reach | 100-280 characters |
| Engagement + saves | 200-280 characters |
| Thread (per tweet) | 240-280 characters |
| Quote tweet commentary | 50-150 characters |

Brevity wins on X. Cut every word that doesn't earn its place. Short punchy tweets outperform verbose ones for reach.

---

### 3. Thread structure

Threads allow longer-form content while keeping each unit readable.

**Thread format:**
```
Tweet 1 (hook): Bold premise + "here's the breakdown:"
Tweet 2-8 (body): One idea per tweet, numbered (1/ 2/ 3/)
Final tweet (CTA): Summary + invite to share/follow
```

**Good thread patterns:**

**The Listicle Thread:**
```
5 counterintuitive things I learned building to $1M:

1/ Speed kills quality.
Shipping fast doesn't mean shipping broken. The best teams move fast AND obsess over craft.

2/ Saying no is the job.
Most founders say yes to everything. The ones who scale say no to 90% of it.
[...etc]

5/ The last thing: relationships compound more than revenue.

Save this if it's useful. Follow for more on building in public.
```

**The Deep Dive Thread:**
```
[Hook tweet]

First, a bit of context: [setup]

Here's what most people miss: [key insight]

The data tells a different story: [evidence]

What this means in practice: [application]

Bottom line: [conclusion + CTA]
```

---

### 4. Engagement mechanics

**What drives replies:** Controversial (but defensible) takes, questions, relatable failures, strong opinions.

**What drives retweets:** Useful frameworks, surprising data, things that make people look smart for sharing.

**What drives likes:** Relatable statements, validating beliefs, emotional moments, humor.

**What drives follows:** Consistent POV on a niche topic + regular demonstration of expertise.

**Effective CTAs:**
- "What would you add?" (invites experts to engage)
- "Am I wrong?" (invites debate — only use if you can back it up)
- "Save this for later"
- "Quote tweet your take"
- "Follow if you found this useful"

Avoid asking for likes or retweets directly — it reads desperate.

---

### 5. Hashtags on X

X's algorithm relies less on hashtags than LinkedIn or Instagram. Use them sparingly:

- **0-2 hashtags** per tweet — max 3 for threads
- Only use hashtags with high-volume communities: `#AI`, `#SaaS`, `#Web3`, `#BuildInPublic`, `#Startup`
- Avoid generic filler: `#motivation`, `#success`, `#entrepreneur` — low signal
- Place hashtags at the end or naturally in text, never mid-sentence

---

### 6. Media on X

**Images:**
- Single image stops the scroll better than a 4-image grid for most use cases
- Text-on-image (white text on dark background) works extremely well
- Aspect ratio: 16:9 for landscape, 1:1 for square, 2:1 for Twitter cards
- Don't add text visible only in the image if the tweet text doesn't mention it — some users won't see the image

**Videos:**
- First 2-3 seconds must hook the viewer
- Optimal length: 30-60 seconds for organic reach
- Always add captions — 70%+ of video is watched with sound off
- Native MP4 uploads get more reach than YouTube links
- Max resolution: 1920×1200 or 1200×1900

**GIFs:**
- Use sparingly — effective for humor or reaction, not for serious topics
- Keep under 5 MB for fast loading

---

### 7. Timing

Best posting times (general, adjust for your audience):
- Weekdays: 8-10am, 12-1pm, 6-8pm in your target timezone
- Tuesday–Thursday tend to have higher engagement
- Avoid Friday evening and weekends for professional/tech topics

For maximum reach, don't post and immediately leave — stay online for 30 minutes to reply to early engagement. The algorithm rewards early interaction.

### 8. High-performing content formats for X

| Format | Why it works |
|--------|-------------|
| Strong opinion with reasoning | Generates debate and replies |
| Personal story with lesson | Relatable, shareable |
| Data or research with your take | Authority + saves |
| "I was wrong about X" | Intellectual honesty, respect |
| Step-by-step breakdown | Saves, follows |
| Behind-the-scenes | Curiosity, authenticity |
| Contrarian takes on mainstream narratives | Engagement magnet |
| Hot takes on recent industry news | Timely, shares |

---

### 9. What to avoid

- **Engagement bait:** "RT if you agree" — platform may suppress these
- **Vague inspiration:** "Work hard and believe" — zero signal, ignored
- **Humble bragging:** Announcing wins without adding value for the reader
- **Over-tagging:** Tagging unrelated people to get attention
- **Repetitive posting:** Same tweet with minor tweaks — the algorithm notices
- **Posting without engaging:** If you never reply to others, your reach suffers
- **Deleting and reposting:** X doesn't favor re-posted content

---

### 10. Reply strategy

Replies to large accounts can drive significant follower growth:

- Reply within the first 30 minutes of a viral tweet
- Add genuine value — a data point, a personal experience, a strong counter-argument
- Keep it to 1-2 sentences — the goal is to spark curiosity to visit your profile
- Never just say "great point" or "agree" — these are invisible

---

### 11. Polls

Polls drive high engagement and are good for:
- Market research ("What's your biggest challenge with X?")
- Fun/relatable comparisons
- Sparking debate on a binary question

Keep poll questions tight. 2 options > 4 options for clear comparison. Duration: 24h for fast results, 3 days for more votes on niche topics.
